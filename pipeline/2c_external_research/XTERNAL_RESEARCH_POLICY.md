# PRISMA — EXTERNAL RESEARCH POLICY (Phase 2C)

VERSION: 1.0  
STATUS: DRAFT  
AUTHORITY LEVEL: SUBORDINATE TO DOCTRINE

---

## PURPOSE

External Research is a controlled, auditable mechanism for contextual enrichment
when internal resources (Doctrine + Evidence) are insufficient.

External Research exists to:
- Fill cultural and historical context gaps
- Surface documented perspectives
- Support mandatory color assignment under uncertainty

External Research is NOT:
- A decision-making authority
- A substitute for Doctrine
- A replacement for Evidence
- A free-form web search

Doctrine remains supreme at all times.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

1. Doctrine constrains all outcomes
2. Evidence is preferred over External Research
3. External Research is contextual, never prescriptive
4. A color assignment MUST always be produced
5. Uncertainty is documented, never used to abstain

---

## FORBIDDEN BEHAVIORS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

- Assigning colors directly
- Recommending a specific Prisma color
- Using pixel, histogram, or dominance language
- Treating consensus as truth
- Using anonymous, social, or AI-generated sources
- Performing general or aesthetic category searches

---

## ALLOWED BEHAVIORS

- Film-specific research only
- Source-attributed perspectives
- Documentation of conflicts
- Translation into Doctrine vocabulary
- Triggering under defined conditions only

---

## TRIGGER CONDITIONS

External Research may only be triggered when:
- Internal Doctrine and Evidence are insufficient to resolve a color assignment or contextual ambiguity.
- Mandatory color assignment is required and uncertainty cannot be resolved by existing resources.
- Editorial review explicitly requests contextual enrichment for audit or documentation purposes.
- All internal evidence queries have been exhausted and documented.
- All internal Doctrine and Evidence queries must be exhausted and explicitly logged before External Research may be triggered.

External Research must never be triggered for general curiosity, aesthetic speculation, or in lieu of proper evidence.

---

## SOURCE AUTHORITY MODEL

All sources used in External Research must be:
- Attributable to a named, published authority (e.g., critic, scholar, filmmaker, institutional publication).
- Cited with full bibliographic detail, including publication date and context.
- Evaluated for relevance to the specific film and color context in question.
- Never anonymous, crowd-sourced, or AI-generated.
- Never used to override or contradict Doctrine.

Editorial discretion may exclude sources deemed unreliable or irrelevant. All inclusions and exclusions must be documented.

All sources must be classified as PRIMARY, SECONDARY, or TERTIARY. Only PRIMARY and SECONDARY sources may materially influence contextual interpretation. TERTIARY sources are allowed for factual background only.

---

## OUTPUT SCHEMA

External Research outputs must include:
- Film identifier and context for which research was performed
- List of all sources consulted, with full citations
- Summary of perspectives and conflicts, translated into Doctrine vocabulary
- Explicit statement of uncertainty and rationale for any unresolved ambiguity
- Editorial notes on why External Research was triggered
- Timestamp and author of research action

All outputs must be structured for audit and traceability. No color assignment or recommendation may be present in the output.

---

## DECISION GUARANTEE

External Research must always result in:
- Sufficient contextual enrichment to enable mandatory color assignment
- Documentation of all uncertainties and conflicts
- Assurance that no abstention or deferral has occurred
- Explicit linkage to the final color assignment decision, with rationale

Failure to produce a decision is a policy violation and must be escalated for editorial review.

Low-quality or limited External Research does NOT block decision-making. In such cases, the system must proceed using Doctrine-only reasoning and adjust confidence accordingly.

---

## RELATION TO EVIDENCE

External Research is subordinate to Evidence and Doctrine at all times.
- Evidence is always preferred and must be exhausted before External Research is triggered.
- External Research may only supplement, never replace, internal evidence.
- All findings from External Research must be contextualized within Doctrine vocabulary and never treated as prescriptive.
- Conflicts between Evidence and External Research must be resolved in favor of Evidence, with rationale documented.

---

## AUDIT & VERSIONING

All External Research actions must be:
- Fully logged with timestamp, author, and rationale
- Versioned for traceability and reproducibility
- Auditable by editorial and technical review at any time
- Retained in the Prisma archive for future reference

Any modification, update, or removal of External Research outputs must be documented and justified. No output may be deleted without explicit editorial approval.
